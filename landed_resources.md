## CHUNK: App Overview

**Tagline:** Your NYC, in your language, for your life.
**Purpose:** A free, multilingual, mobile-first web app that gives NYC newcomers a personalized guide to getting settled — in their language, for their situation.

### Fair Fares NYC — 50% Discount Transit

**category:** transit
**personas:** immigrant, refugee, neighbor
**is_free:** true

50% off subway, bus, and Access-A-Ride for low-income NYC residents ages 18–64. Immigration status is NOT required.

**Income limits (2025):**
- Individual: $22,692/year (145% Federal Poverty Level)
- Family of 4: $46,617/year
- If you already receive SNAP or Cash Assistance, you likely qualify automatically

**How to apply:**
1. Go to https://www.nyc.gov/site/fairfares/index.page
2. Or apply via ACCESS HRA: https://access.nyc.gov/programs/fair-fares/
3. Download ACCESS HRA app (iOS / Android) to upload documents
4. No interview required — processed within 30 days
5. New enrollees receive a Fair Fares OMNY card by mail

**Documents needed:**
- Photo ID (IDNYC, passport, driver's license)
- Proof of NYC address (utility bill, lease, or official mail from last 60 days)
- Proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns, or employer letter)

**Phone:** 311
**Office hours:** Mon–Fri 8:30am–5pm
**Office locations:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/fairfares/contact-us/contact-us.page
**FAQs:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/fairfares/faq/faq.page

### NYC Care — Free/Low-Cost Health Program

**category:** health
**personas:** immigrant, refugee, all without insurance
**is_free:** true

NYC Care gives you access to your own doctor and services at little or no cost. Available to all NYC residents who don't qualify for or can't afford health insurance, regardless of immigration status.

- Fees start at $0 for those earning under 200% FPL (~$31,000/year individual)
- 70+ patient care locations across all 5 boroughs
- Gives you a membership card, regular doctor, affordable prescriptions
- This is NOT insurance — it is a city health access program

**Website:** https://www.nyccare.nyc/
**Enroll:** https://www.nyccare.nyc/enroll/
**Locations:** https://www.nyccare.nyc/locations/
**Phone:** 1-646-NYC-CARE (1-646-692-2273)
**Hours:** Daily — call to enroll or visit any NYC H+H location
**Languages:** 250+ languages via interpretation services

**NYC Health + Hospitals main site:** https://www.nychealthandhospitals.org/

### SNAP — Food Stamps

**category:** food
**personas:** immigrant (check eligibility), neighbor, student
**is_free:** true

SNAP provides an EBT card that works like a debit card at grocery stores.

- Apply online, by mail, or at an HRA office
- Emergency SNAP available in 5–7 days if in crisis
- Expedited SNAP if income under $150/month or housing costs exceed income

**Apply:** https://access.nyc.gov/programs/snap/ or https://mybenefits.ny.gov
**ACCESS HRA (apply online):** https://a069-access.nyc.gov/accesshra/
**Phone:** HRA Infoline: 718-557-1399 | 311
**HRA food assistance page:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/hra/help/food-assistance.page
**Hours:** Online 24/7; HRA offices Mon–Fri 8am–5pm

## CHUNK: Schools — Enrolling Children

**category:** school
**personas:** immigrant, refugee

> Every child ages 4–21 has a RIGHT to free public education in NYC regardless of immigration status. Schools CANNOT ask for immigration status. A child must be placed within 5 school days of application.

---

### How to Enroll in NYC Public Schools

**Main enrollment website:** https://www.schools.nyc.gov/enrollment
**School search (find your zoned school):** https://schoolsearch.schools.nyc.gov/
**Family Welcome Centers:** https://www.schools.nyc.gov/enrollment/enrollment-help/family-welcome-centers
**Phone:** 718-935-2009, Mon–Fri 8am–6pm
**Immigrant families guide:** https://www.schools.nyc.gov/school-life/school-environment/immigrant-families

**Documents needed:**
- Proof of age (birth certificate or passport)
- 2 proofs of NYC address (lease, utility bill, official mail)
- Immunization records

**No documents? No problem.** Schools must enroll temporarily even without all documents. Homeless children enroll without any documentation.

**English Language Learners (ELL):** All schools offer English as a New Language (ENL) support. Children are tested within 10 days of enrollment.

- Apply via: https://www.myschools.nyc

**MySchools.nyc languages:** English, Arabic, Bangla, Chinese, Haitian Creole, French, Korean, Russian, Spanish, Urdu

## CHUNK: Legal Rights & Immigration

**category:** legal
**personas:** immigrant, refugee

### Your Rights — Key Facts

- **Sanctuary City:** NYC will not ask about immigration status for most city services. City employees must keep status confidential.
- **ICE at your door:** You do NOT have to open the door. Ask if they have a judicial warrant signed by a judge. If no judicial warrant, do not let them in.
- **ICE at the hospital:** ICE cannot enter private areas without a judicial warrant. Hospitals do not share information with ICE.
- **Police stop:** You have the right to remain silent. Ask if you are free to go.
- **Schools:** ICE cannot enter schools for immigration enforcement without a judicial warrant. DOE does not track or share immigration status.
- **Work rights:** All workers regardless of status have rights to minimum wage, safe conditions, and protection from retaliation.
- **Know your rights:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/immigrants/city-services/city-services-summary.page

## CHUNK: Housing & Renter Rights

**category:** housing
**personas:** immigrant, refugee, neighbor

### Key Renter Rights in NYC

- **Heat:** Landlord must provide heat (68°F daytime, Oct 1–May 31) and hot water year-round
- **Repairs:** Landlord must fix conditions affecting health/safety. File with HPD if they refuse.
- **Eviction:** Landlord CANNOT lock you out or remove your belongings without a court order. This is illegal (illegal lockout).
- **No discrimination:** Landlord cannot discriminate based on national origin, source of income, or immigration status
- **Rent stabilization:** Check if your apartment qualifies at https://hcr.ny.gov

---

### Housing Resources

**Report housing violations (heat, pests, mold, repairs)**
- Call: 311 (24/7, 200+ languages)
- Online: https://portal.311.nyc.gov

**HPD — Housing Preservation & Development**
- Website: https://www.nyc.gov/site/hpd/index.page
- Phone: 212-863-5620
- File complaints, look up building violations

**NYC Affordable Housing Lottery — Housing Connect**
- Website: https://housingconnect.nyc.gov
- Apply early and often. Income-based. Lottery system.

**NYC Rent Guidelines Board (check if rent-stabilized)**

**Emergency Shelter (DHS)**
- Call: 311 (24/7)

## CHUNK: Banking & Money

**category:** banking
**personas:** immigrant, refugee

### IDNYC — Free NYC Municipal ID

**category:** banking
**personas:** all
**is_free:** true

Free photo ID for anyone age 10+ who lives in NYC. No immigration status required.
Accepted at banks, government offices, museums, and libraries.
Comes with free 1-year memberships to major museums (MoMA, Brooklyn Museum, etc.)

**Website:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/idnyc/index.page
**Apply / make appointment:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/idnyc/locations/locations.page
**Phone:** 311 and say "IDNYC"

---

### Banking Without a Social Security Number

You can open a bank account with an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) or a foreign passport + IDNYC.

| Bank / Service | Account Type | What You Need |
|---|---|---|
| Bank of America SafeBalance | No-fee checking | ITIN or foreign passport + IDNYC |
| Citibank Access Account | Basic checking, no overdraft | Foreign passport + proof of address |
| Chase Secure Banking | No overdraft, $4.95/mo | ITIN or foreign passport |
| Local credit unions | Flexible requirements | IDNYC + proof of address usually enough |
| Remitly | Send money internationally | Phone number + ID |
| Wise | International transfers, low fees | Phone number + ID |

**Get an ITIN (no SSN required):** https://www.irs.gov/individuals/individual-taxpayer-identification-number

## CHUNK: Work & Employment Rights

**category:** work
**personas:** immigrant, refugee, neighbor

> All workers in NYC have rights regardless of immigration status — including minimum wage, safe working conditions, and protection from retaliation.

### Worker Rights at a Glance (2025–2026)

| Right | Details |
|---|---|
| Minimum wage | $16.50/hr citywide; fast food workers $17.50/hr |
| Overtime | 1.5x pay for hours over 40/week (most workers) |
| Paid sick leave | Up to 56 hours/year (employers with 100+ employees) |
| Safe workplace | OSHA protections apply to all workers |
| No retaliation | Employer cannot fire/threaten you for reporting violations |
| Wage theft | Employer must pay all wages owed |
